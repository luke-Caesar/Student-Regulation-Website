Chapter 4: Offenses and Categories

This chapter explains the different levels of violations. There is Three different levels of violations in order of severity from least to worst:

  1. Mild Offenses
  2. Moderate Offenses or Repeated Mild Offenses
  3. Severe Offenses or Repeated Moderate Offenses

List of Mild Offenses:

  • Disrupting class order, including during online learning sessions.
  • Arriving late to school or entering classlate.
  • Failing to bring necessary learning materials.
  • Using school facilities outside school hours without permission.
  • Wearing inappropriate or excessive accessories.
  • Eating in school areas outside of designated mealtimes without permission.
  • Turning on and/or using mobile phones or other electronic devices during school hours without permission.
  • Bringing and/or playing games orsports equipment without permission in school areas or events.
  • Engaging in any formof buying orselling transactions (goods or services) on school premises.
  • Failing to wear the properschool uniform.
  • Bringing and parking bicycles,motorcycles, or electric vehicles in school areas without permission.

List of Moderate Offenses:

  • Causing damage to school facilities. The studentmust replace or repair the damaged item.
  • Leaving school orschool-related activities during active hours without proper permission.
  • Misusing school facilitiessuch as projectorremotes, air conditioning controls, elevators, etc.
  • Damaging,misusing,orremoving officialschool announcements.
  • Celebrating birthdays, promotions, or graduations beyond reasonable limits atschool.
  • Being involved in lending or borrowing money atschool.
  • Neglecting to participate in school activities without valid reasons.

List of Severe Offenses:

  • Engaging in bullying in any form—verbal,physical,written, or visual—that is threatening or harassing, especially if repeated. Bullying is defined as intentional, aggressive behavior that is repeated over time and involves an imbalance of power. It can be physical, verbal, social, or digital (cyberbullying).
  • Defaming the school’s name in any formorthrough any medium.
  • Being involved in physical fights either inside or outside of school.
  • Cheating during examsin any form.
  • Plagiarizing other's work without permission.
  • Bringing sharp weapons or dangerous objects.
  • Engaging in or possessing pornographic content or indecent behavior.
  • Possessing or consuming alcoholic beverages or cigarettes, either on or off school premises.
  • Possessing or using illegal drugs or controlled substances.
  • Gambling in any form or game.
  • Threatening or harassing teachers,staff,orfellow students in any manner.
  • Taking someone else’s belongings without permission.
  • Being involved in youth delinquency groups or prohibited organizations.
  • Engaging in criminal acts, whether on or offschool premises.
  • Falsifying or manipulating school documents or information.
  • Participating in or organizing school-related activities outside the school without permission.
  • Involving external partiesto resolve school-related issues without school approval.

If any violation not listed above occurs, the school reservesthe right to address it according to established school policies.
